\subsection{Results on Synthetic Graphs} \label{subsec6_synthetic}
\subsubsection{Temporal Stochastic Block Model: TSBM}
To assess the robustness and generalizability of the proposed \IMPaCT\ methods under assumptions 1–3, we conducted experiments using synthetic graphs generated from the Temporal Stochastic Block Model (TSBM), a temporally extended variant of the classical SBM \cite{holland1983stochastic,deshpande2018contextual}. Whereas the standard SBM characterizes edge formation through a community-level probability matrix $\mathbf{P}_{y\tilde y}$, TSBM generalizes this formulation by introducing a $4$-dimensional tensor $\mathbf{P}_{t\tilde t y\tilde y}$ that captures dependencies on both node labels and timestamps.

The data generation procedure was designed to satisfy all target assumptions. Node features $\mathbf{x}_i \in \mathbb{R}^f$ were sampled conditionally on labels according to $\mathbf{x}_i = \mu(y_i) + k_{y_i} Z_i$, where $Z_i$ are IID Gaussian random vectors. Labels and timestamps were generated independently. Temporal edge formation was governed by an exponentially decaying connectivity function with decay parameter $\gamma_{y,\tilde y}$, defined by
\begin{align}
    g(y, \tilde y, |t - \tilde t|) 
    = \gamma_{y,\tilde y}^{|t - \tilde t|} \, g(y, \tilde y, 0), 
    \quad \forall |t - \tilde t| > 0.
\end{align}

\subsubsection{Experimental Setup for Synthetic Graphs}

We adopt Simple Graph Convolution (SGC) \cite{SGC} as the primary decoupled GNN baseline for synthetic experiments. To further examine whether the proposed methods improve more general spatial GNN architectures, we additionally include a two-layer GCN \cite{gcn} with averaging message passing. We apply \UMP{}, \SMP{}, \SMP{}+\ASA{}, and \SMP{}+\JJnorm{} to both baselines. Since GCN performs nonlinear semantic aggregation, \JJnorm{} is applied at each layer rather than solely at the final representation layer.

Moreover, to evaluate the generalizability of \JJnorm, which is derived under assumptions 4 and 5, we conducted experiments on synthetic graphs constructed to either satisfy or violate these assumptions. In particular, for graphs satisfying assumption 5, we introduce a temporal decay factor $\gamma$ governing edge formation, where smaller values of $\gamma$ correspond to more rapidly decaying connection probabilities. We further analyze performance trends across varying values of $\gamma$. Detailed experimental configurations are provided in Appendix~\ref{apdx:synthetic_setup}. The corresponding results are summarized in Table~\ref{table:synthetic} and illustrated in Figure~\ref{fig:synthetic}.
